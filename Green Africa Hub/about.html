Our Framework

The Smart-Prep Methodology

Evidence-based techniques that reduce cooking time, energy, and cost — without compromising authenticity or flavour.

Induction-First Cooking

Every recipe is optimised for induction hobs — precise temperature control, faster heat-up times, and 85-90% energy efficiency compared to 40-55% for gas. We teach specific power levels and timing for each dish.

Batch & Prep Strategies

Strategic batch cooking, pressure cooking, and prep sequencing that cut energy use per serving. Cook once, eat multiple meals — without the food getting boring.

Time & Temperature Precision

Every recipe includes exact induction power levels and timing. No more guesswork — just consistent results that save energy and deliver perfect flavour every time.

Carbon Badge Tracking

Every dish gets a Carbon Badge — a full lifecycle audit covering food production, transport, and cooking energy. You know exactly what your meal costs the planet.

Our Principle

British Alternative Ingredients

Tradition first. Substitution only where the planet demands it.

"We only substitute air-freighted ingredients."

All sea-shipped authentic ingredients remain exactly as tradition intended. Rice, yam, plantain, dried fish, palm oil, ground crayfish — these travel by container ship at a fraction of the carbon cost of air freight. We never touch them.

The only ingredients we swap are those that must be air-freighted because they perish too quickly for sea transport. Fresh leafy vegetables like ugwu (fluted pumpkin leaf) are the prime example — they must fly from Nigeria to the UK, creating enormous carbon emissions per kilogram.

🔄 Ugwu → British Spinach

Used in Pounded Yam & Spinach Lamb Stew. Transport carbon reduction: 99.9%. Same nutritional profile, same green colour, same cooking method.

🔄 Ugwu → British Kale

Used in Kale Soup with Fufu. Transport carbon reduction: 99.9%. Kale's robust texture works beautifully in traditional soup recipes.

Transparency

The Carbon Badge Framework

Full lifecycle carbon accounting for every recipe. Here's how it works.

🌾

Food Production

CO₂e per kilogram for each ingredient, using UK Government GHG Conversion Factors and peer-reviewed lifecycle assessments. Covers farming, processing, and packaging.

🚢

Ingredient Transport

Sea freight (~16g CO₂/tonne-km) vs air freight (~500g CO₂/tonne-km). We calculate actual routes from source country to UK for each ingredient. Most African staples ship by sea.

⚡

Cooking Energy

Measured energy consumption per recipe on both induction and gas hobs. Uses UK grid intensity (124g CO₂/kWh, 2024) and natural gas emission factor (215g CO₂/kWh).
