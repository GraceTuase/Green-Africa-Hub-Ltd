Why Green Africa Hub Exists

The green transition has left our communities behind

Many African and Caribbean households are facing rising cooking costs, pressure on traditional food practices, and a green transition that has not been designed with our communities in mind.

Green Africa Hub responds to that challenge with practical, culturally grounded solutions that help people save energy, preserve heritage, and build greener futures through food.

We believe sustainability should work in everyday kitchens — not just in policy documents or mainstream food systems.

Rising cooking costs

African and Caribbean households face energy bills up to 40% above the national average, with traditional gas cooking a major driver of fuel poverty.

Food traditions under pressure

The rising cost of cooking and a lack of community-specific green guidance risks eroding the food traditions that connect families to their heritage.

A green transition not built for us

Sustainability solutions have been designed largely for mainstream food culture. African and Caribbean communities deserve tools built specifically for them.

How Our Model Works

Practical tools for real kitchens

We combine hands-on learning, smarter cooking methods, and accessible sustainability tools — all built around African and Caribbean food culture.

In Person

Smart Kitchen Learning

We run practical cooking and sustainability sessions that show how traditional African and Caribbean meals can be prepared in more energy-efficient ways — without losing flavour, culture, or confidence.

Methodology

Smart-Prep Methods

Our Smart-Prep approach helps reduce wasted time, wasted energy, and unnecessary cost — while protecting authentic flavour and the cooking traditions that matter most to our communities.

Technology

Carbon-Aware Tools

We are building accessible tools that help households and food businesses understand the environmental impact of their cooking choices — in a way that is practical, culturally relevant, and easy to act on.

The Founder

Grace Omotuase
CEO & Founder

Green Africa Hub was founded by Grace Omotuase, who brings together leadership in finance, technology, sustainability, and food education.

Her work combines community insight with practical innovation — creating solutions that support both cultural preservation and climate action.

Under her leadership, Green Africa Hub is building a model that starts in Bristol and is designed to grow into other communities over time.
