🌍 Our Mission: Electric Induction Transition

Evidence-based transition from gas to electric induction hobs in African Caribbean communities

Gas cookers: 2.5-3.8kg CO2 • Electric induction: 1.2-1.3kg CO2 (60% reduction)

"Sustainability is everyone's business — and should be the business of everyone. Black communities are not just part of the green transition. We are leading it."

Food traditions under pressure

The rising cost of cooking and a lack of community-specific green guidance risks eroding the food traditions that connect families to their heritage.

A green transition not built for us

Sustainability solutions have been designed largely for mainstream food culture. African and Caribbean communities deserve tools built specifically for them.

How Our Model Works

Practical tools for real kitchens

We combine hands-on learning, smarter cooking methods, and accessible sustainability tools — all built around African and Caribbean food culture.

In Person

Smart Kitchen Learning

We run practical cooking and sustainability sessions that show how traditional African and Caribbean meals can be prepared with electric hub cookers — reducing energy costs by up to 60%, eliminating gas emissions, and preserving authentic flavour and cultural heritage.

Methodology

Smart-Prep Methods

Our Smart-Prep approach reduces wasted time, energy, and costs while protecting authentic flavour. We teach bulk cooking, food waste elimination, and sustainable packaging solutions that make communities more resilient and environmentally responsible.

Technology

Carbon-Aware Tools

We provide real-time carbon tracking, cooking footprint badges, and sustainability analytics that help households and food businesses understand their environmental impact — making it easy to transition from gas to electric cooking while saving money and protecting heritage.

The Founder

Grace Omotuase
CEO & Founder

Green Africa Hub was founded by Grace Omotuase, who brings together leadership in finance, technology, sustainability, and food education.

Her work combines community insight with practical innovation — creating solutions that support both cultural preservation and climate action.

Under her leadership, Green Africa Hub is building a model that starts in Bristol and is designed to grow into other communities over time.
