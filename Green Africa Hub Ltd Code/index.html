The Challenge

Why This Matters

African and Caribbean communities face unique barriers in the transition to sustainable cooking.

01

Rising Cooking Costs

African and Caribbean households face energy bills 40% above the national average. Traditional cooking methods on gas are both expensive and carbon-intensive.

02

Food Traditions Under Pressure

Key ingredients are air-freighted at enormous carbon cost. Cultural recipes risk being labelled "unsustainable" when the real issue is supply chain, not the food itself.

03

A Green Transition Not Built for Us

Sustainability advice rarely considers African and Caribbean cooking styles. We need solutions designed for our kitchens, our ingredients, and our communities.

Our Approach

1

In-Person Sessions

Meet the Founder

Grace Omotuase

Chartered Manager · Data Scientist · Professional Cook

With 14 years of financial leadership — including 12 years at the Central Bank of Nigeria — Grace brings rigorous data analysis to the kitchen. After 18 months of pre-incorporation research, she founded Green Africa Hub to prove that heritage cooking and sustainability belong together.

Grace holds certifications in Chartered Management, Data Science, and professional cooking. She combines financial modelling with hands-on kitchen expertise to build a social enterprise that's as evidence-based as it is delicious.
