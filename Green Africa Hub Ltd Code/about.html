Our Story

About Green Africa Hub

A social enterprise born from the belief that African and Caribbean food heritage holds the key to sustainable living — for our communities and our planet.

The Founder

Grace Omotuase
CEO & Founder

Grace Omotuase is a Chartered Manager, Data Scientist, and sustainability entrepreneur based in Bristol. She holds an MSc in Financial Technology from the University of Bradford, a Data Science Certificate from the University of Texas at Austin, a Professional Cookery qualification, and CIMA Management Level — alongside 14 years of financial leadership including 12 years at the Central Bank of Nigeria, managing teams of 30+ staff and overseeing programmes exceeding £300 million.

Grace personally developed the proprietary Smart-Prep methodology and AI carbon tracking dashboard during 18 months of pre-incorporation research and development. She brings a rare combination of quantitative expertise, cultural credibility, professional cookery training, and deep community roots — making Green Africa Hub a first-of-its-kind venture in the UK.

Completing the WECA Sustainability in Business Bootcamp at Future Leap in March 2026 was a pivotal moment. It crystallised Grace's vision of what was truly possible — and instantly sharpened and reshaped her business goals. What had begun as a food and energy project became something far more purposeful: a community-centred, technically driven, and R&D-led sustainability enterprise. The Bootcamp gave Grace the institutional context, policy grounding, and professional network to see the future of sustainability clearly — and to build Green Africa Hub around it with intention and confidence.

Purpose

Mission, Vision & Values

Our Mission

To ensure African and Caribbean communities lead — not follow — the UK's green transition, protecting cultural heritage while reducing energy costs and carbon emissions. Sustainability is everyone's business — and should be the business of everyone.

Our Vision

A world where African and Caribbean cooking is affordable, green, and future-proof — and where our communities are globally recognised as leaders of the sustainable food movement, not bystanders to it.

Our Values

Heritage first. Scientific rigour. Radical honesty. Community over competition. We build trust through transparency — from our carbon calculations to our business model and our partnerships.
